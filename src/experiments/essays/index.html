CESRI: The Formulaic Way of Writing Analytical Essays


Dante Tam, 11/30/2017


For when you're writing your analytic, argumentative, thesis-based essays, here's a quick way to structure your arguments. This is so you can focus on thinking about arguments,
and not about the tedious details of essay organization. I feel that essay structure is taught too infrequently in high schools and colleges.

The general idea: move from coarse, observational, to fine-tuned, and analytical. Here's the CESRI structure:

Claim: the point you are proving or the analysis you are outlining. This should relate back to and directly support your thesis.

     The industrial processes of food production distance people from their nourishment, both literally and in a sociological sense.

 

Evidence: the exact piece of evidence you are citing and analyzing. Choose evidence from a reputable source that provides room for expansion and deep thought.

     In the New York Times article "When a Crop Becomes King",
     Michael Pollan details the widespread (mis)uses of corn in nearly all modern food products... (remember to cite quotes properly)

 

Summary: an optional place to clarify, emphasize, or detail your evidence - important for citing large quotes.

     Pollan details the massive food production industries built around corn cultivation.

 

Reasoning: the deep analysis of the evidence in support of your claim. This is the meat of your essay, and where you shine and show off your brilliant mind.

     Pollan uses corn as a vehicle to analyze the greater industrialization of American food production,
     and the economic structures necessary to support a modern society of heavy consumption.

 

Interpretation: the "extra" analysis of your points. Being the last impression you leave on a reader,
this is the perfect time to add your opinions, significances, relations to course materials, expansions to further analysis, and transitions to establish your next points.
This is also a great way to add to your unique "voice".

     I find these hideous structures of agricultural power as a necessary evil in a modern convenience-based society...
     [...which has created the great distance in food and people we have studied in this course.]
     [...which has so greatly diverged from natural existence.]
     [...which heavily relies on commercial brands to establish quick, artificial bonds of trust between consumers and producers. This is discussed in Pillsbury, where...]

 

And that's it. Simply follow this formula all the way to proving your point.
Develop a good point, find good evidence, figure out how it proves your point, and then prove your point.
Bonus points for strong verbs, specific nouns, descriptive adjectives, flowing transitions, and unique voice.
The structure is open to interpretation. Write for your own enjoyment.
